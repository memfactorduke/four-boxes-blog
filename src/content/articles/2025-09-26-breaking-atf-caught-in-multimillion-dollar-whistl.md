## Summary

Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman Charles Grassley has transmitted a formal letter to Attorney General Pam Bondi and the Acting Director of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF), detailing explosive whistleblower disclosures regarding a longstanding, systematic, and allegedly illegal practice by the ATF of deliberately misclassifying human resources and administrative positions as law enforcement positions — resulting in the improper payment of enhanced law enforcement compensation and benefits to individuals not entitled to receive them, at a cost to taxpayers of millions of dollars. The same whistleblower, whose allegations were fully substantiated by the United States Office of Special Counsel, has allegedly been subjected to retaliation by the ATF for his disclosures, in violation of federal whistleblower protection statutes.

## Background

The Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives has long been the subject of oversight concerns regarding its internal management practices and its treatment of employees who raise legitimate concerns about agency operations. The present matter involves an ATF employee and human resources professional, Mr. Siwa Jula, who came forward several years ago to disclose a pattern of conduct that he characterized as systematic misclassification of non-law enforcement positions within the agency as law enforcement positions.

The practical consequence of this misclassification scheme, according to the whistleblower's disclosures, was that individuals occupying human resources, administrative, and other non-enforcement roles at the ATF were compensated at the higher pay grades, enhanced benefit levels, and superior retirement packages associated with law enforcement status — classifications to which they were not entitled under the applicable civil service regulations. The United States Office of Personnel Management (OPM), which bears supervisory responsibility over federal agency personnel classifications, suspended the ATF's authority to classify certain positions pending resolution of the misclassification issues.

## The Whistleblower's Disclosures and OSC Validation

What makes this matter particularly significant from a legal and constitutional accountability standpoint is that the whistleblower's allegations were not merely colorable or plausible — they were fully substantiated by the United States Office of Special Counsel (OSC), the independent federal agency responsible for investigating whistleblower disclosures and protecting federal employees who report misconduct.

In a letter from OSC Director Henry Kerner, the Office formally recognized Mr. Jula's contribution to uncovering the ATF's misconduct, stating: "As a human resources professional at the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives, you were instrumental in bringing to light ATF's long-standing and illegal practice of deliberately misclassifying upper-level law enforcement positions to put unqualified individuals in those positions at significant taxpayer expense. You persisted in raising these concerns to agency management despite the entrenched culture at the agency of disregarding civil service rules and regulations."

The OSC further noted that Mr. Jula's disclosures led to the suspension of the ATF's classification authority and directed the agency to investigate and remedy the misclassification issues. The OSC awarded Mr. Jula a formal commendation for his service in exposing the misconduct.

## ATF Retaliation Against the Whistleblower

Notwithstanding the full substantiation of his disclosures by the OSC and the formal recognition of his contributions, Mr. Jula has reportedly continued to face retaliation from ATF colleagues and supervisors angered by his whistleblowing activities. Senator Grassley's letter to Attorney General Bondi and the ATF Acting Director details specific allegations of retaliatory conduct and requests a full accounting of the steps taken to protect Mr. Jula from further retaliation and to discipline those responsible for the improper conduct he exposed.

Federal law provides robust protections for government whistleblowers. The Whistleblower Protection Act, 5 U.S.C. § 2302, prohibits any federal agency from taking a "personnel action" against an employee because of the employee's disclosure of information that the employee reasonably believes evidences a violation of law, rule, or regulation; gross mismanagement; a gross waste of funds; an abuse of authority; or a substantial and specific danger to public health or safety. Retaliation against a whistleblower whose disclosures have been substantiated by the OSC — and who has been formally recognized for those disclosures — would represent a particularly egregious violation of these protections.

## Senator Grassley's Formal Inquiry

Chairman Grassley's letter to Attorney General Bondi requests a comprehensive response to several questions, including: what steps the DOJ and ATF have taken to address the misclassification issues identified by the whistleblower; what disciplinary actions, if any, have been taken against individuals responsible for the misclassification scheme; what measures have been implemented to protect Mr. Jula from ongoing retaliation; and what plans are in place to recover the taxpayer funds that were improperly expended as a result of the misclassification scheme.

The involvement of the Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman — a figure whose institutional credibility and record of effective oversight is well established — brings considerable leverage to bear on these questions. Senator Grassley has a long and distinguished record of conducting rigorous oversight of federal agencies and compelling accountability for misconduct, and I expect that his involvement will produce meaningful responses from the DOJ and the ATF.

## Legal and Constitutional Dimensions

The ATF misclassification scheme raises issues of broader constitutional and legal significance beyond the immediate question of fiscal accountability. The deliberate misclassification of non-law enforcement personnel as law enforcement personnel — with the attendant compensation and benefits — suggests not merely administrative sloppiness but an organizational culture within the ATF that treats federal civil service rules as obstacles to be circumvented rather than legal requirements to be observed.

This is not a trivial matter. The ATF is the primary federal agency charged with enforcement of the nation's firearms laws, including the regulation of federally licensed firearms dealers and manufacturers. An agency that demonstrates a willingness to circumvent the civil service rules governing its own personnel — and then to retaliate against the employee who exposed that circumvention — raises legitimate concerns about whether that agency's enforcement activities are conducted with the lawfulness and integrity that the public and the regulated community are entitled to expect.

## Implications

Senator Grassley's investigation and Chairman Grassley's letter to Attorney General Bondi represent an important exercise of congressional oversight that I believe will have meaningful consequences. The substantiated whistleblower disclosures provide a documented evidentiary record of the ATF's misconduct, and the involvement of the Senate Judiciary Committee creates institutional accountability that transcends any single administration.

For the Second Amendment community, this episode is instructive on a different level. The ATF's institutional culture — demonstrated here through the misclassification scheme, the retaliation against a whistleblower who exposed it, and the continuation of that retaliation even after the OSC validated the whistleblower's disclosures — is deeply relevant to evaluating the agency's fitness to serve as the primary regulatory authority over the nation's firearms industry. An agency with this track record of internal misconduct and contempt for civil service rules cannot reasonably expect to be accorded uncritical deference in its enforcement and regulatory activities affecting the rights of law-abiding gun owners.
