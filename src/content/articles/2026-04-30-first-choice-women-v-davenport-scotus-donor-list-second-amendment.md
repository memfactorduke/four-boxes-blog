The Supreme Court just handed the anti-gun crowd a 9-0 loss that has nothing to do with the Second Amendment on its face — and everything to do with the Second Amendment in practice.

The case is *First Choice Women's Resource Centers, Inc. v. Davenport* (formerly *v. Platkin*, before Matthew Platkin was replaced as New Jersey's Attorney General). On April 29, 2026, a unanimous Court reversed the Third Circuit and held that pro-life pregnancy centers in New Jersey have Article III standing to challenge a state subpoena demanding their donor lists in federal court. That sounds like a niche pro-life ruling. It is not. It is a precedent the Second Amendment community is going to use, and use hard.

Here is what happened. After *Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization* (2022) overturned *Roe v. Wade*, then–New Jersey AG Matthew Platkin issued a sweeping subpoena to First Choice Women's Resource Centers, a network of faith-based pregnancy counseling centers. Among other things, the subpoena demanded the names of the people who donate money to the organization. First Choice went to federal court and asked a federal judge to enjoin the state from enforcing the subpoena, on the ground that turning over the donor list violates the First Amendment rights of free speech, association, and petition.

The federal district court tossed the case. The Third Circuit, in a divided panel, affirmed. Both courts said First Choice lacked standing because it had not yet been "injured" — the theory being that no one had been forced to comply, and the state had not yet gone into state court to enforce the subpoena.

Wrong, says the Supreme Court. 9-0.

The Court's reasoning is the part that matters for us. The unanimous opinion held that the subpoena itself — the mere demand for donor information — caused an injury in fact. Why? Because that kind of compelled-disclosure demand chills donors. People stop giving when they think the state is going to find out who they are and what they support. That chill is the injury. It is fairly traceable to the AG's subpoena. And a federal court can fix it by enjoining enforcement. That is all three Article III standing elements: injury in fact, causation, redressability. Done.

Now read the language the Court actually used:

> "The First Amendment guarantees all Americans the rights to speak, worship, publish, assemble, and petition their government freely. Each of these rights, this Court has long understood, necessarily carries with it a corresponding right to associate with others. Without such a right, no two men could safely share the same soapbox, no two women the same church. The government could reduce any assembly to a party of one, and the right to petition would amount to nothing more than the power to sign one's own name."

That is the Court reaffirming the freedom of association as the structural backbone of the rest of the First Amendment. And then this passage, leaning directly on the unanimous 1958 decision in *NAACP v. Alabama*:

> "Compelled disclosure of affiliation with groups engaged in advocacy can constitute an effective restraint on the freedom of association."

The Court goes further. Those harms are "particularly likely to follow when the government seeks to intrude into the workings of groups that hold dissident beliefs disfavored by those holding the reins of power."

That sentence is the gold. Read it again with Second Amendment eyes. "Dissident beliefs disfavored by those holding the reins of power" describes every gun owner and every pro-2A donor in California, New York, New Jersey, Illinois, and the rest of the blue-state map. We are the dissident minority in those jurisdictions. We are the people whose donors get harassed when their names get out. We are the ones whose contributions to the NRA, the Second Amendment Foundation, the Firearms Policy Coalition, and Gun Owners of America make us targets when the wrong AG decides to fish through the records.

That is why this 9-0 unanimous precedent is going on the shelf in every Second Amendment lawyer's office in the country. The next time a blue-state AG sends a subpoena demanding a 2A organization's donor list, the response writes itself: *First Choice*. The injury is the chill. The chill is real. Federal court has jurisdiction. Stand down.

The only way the other side wins the gun argument is by cutting off our ability to make the argument. Cut off the donors, you cut off the lawyers, the litigation, the speech, the channels, the organizations. That is the play. The Supreme Court just made that play a lot harder.

Without the soap box, the cartridge box does not survive. That is why this First Amendment win is also a Second Amendment win.
